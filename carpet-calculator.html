How to Calculate Carpet for Your Room

Carpet is typically sold by the square yard, and installation requires careful planning to minimize waste and seams. Our calculator helps you determine exactly how much carpet you need.

Carpet Measurement Basics

  • Square Yards: Carpet is priced per square yard (9 sq ft = 1 sq yd)
  • Roll Width: Most carpet comes in 12ft or 15ft widths
  • Seam Planning: Minimize seams for better appearance
  • Waste Factor: Add 5-10% for cutting and fitting

Common Carpet Roll Widths

Roll Width Best For Typical Use
12 ft Standard rooms up to 12ft wide Most residential installations
15 ft Wider rooms without seams Large living rooms, master bedrooms
13.5 ft Broadloom carpet High-end residential
6 ft Runners and narrow spaces Hallways, stairs

Seam Planning Strategy

Proper seam planning can save money and improve appearance:

  • Avoid high-traffic seams: Place seams in low-traffic areas
  • Match pile direction: Ensure carpet pile runs the same direction
  • Plan for patterns: Pattern matching may increase waste
  • Consider room layout: Furniture placement can hide seams

Carpet Calculator FAQ

How do I convert square feet to square yards?

Divide square feet by 9. For example, 180 sq ft ÷ 9 = 20 square yards.

What's the difference between carpet and padding?

Carpet is the visible floor covering. Padding (underlay) goes underneath for comfort, insulation, and carpet longevity.

How much waste should I add for carpet?

Add 5-10% for standard installations, 10-15% for complex room shapes or pattern matching.

Can I avoid seams in my carpet?

If your room's width is less than the carpet roll width, you can avoid seams. For wider rooms, plan seam placement carefully.
